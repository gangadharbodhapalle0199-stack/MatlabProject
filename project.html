🎯 Aim of the Experiment

🔬 Experiment Objective

The aim of this virtual lab is to study one dimensional steady-state heat conduction through a solid wall or rod and to calculate parameters such as heat flux, rate of heat flow, thermal conductivity, and temperature at a particular point.

🎓 Learning Outcomes

By completing this lab, you will be able to:

  • Understand the concept of one dimensional heat flow.
  • Apply Fourier's law of heat conduction to real problems.
  • Calculate heat flux and rate of heat transfer.
  • Determine thermal conductivity from given data.
  • Find temperature at any location in a wall under steady-state conditions.

🧰 Virtual Apparatus

  • Metallic rod with uniform cross-section
  • Heater at one end, heat sink (cooler) at the other end
  • Thermocouples to measure temperature at multiple points
  • Insulation around the rod to ensure one dimensional flow
📖 Theory — Heat Conduction Concepts

🔥 What is Heat Conduction?

Heat conduction is the transfer of thermal energy through a material without any bulk movement of the material itself. It happens because energetic molecules transfer energy to neighbouring less-energetic molecules.

📏 One Dimensional Heat Flow

When heat flows only in one direction (e.g., through the thickness of a wall or along the length of a rod), it is called one dimensional conduction. Temperature varies only along that direction.

⚖️ Steady-State Conduction

In steady-state, the temperature at every point inside the material does not change with time. Heat flowing in equals heat flowing out. For a uniform rod with no heat generation, the temperature profile is linear.

📐 Fourier's Law

The rate of heat transfer is proportional to the temperature gradient and the cross-sectional area:

q = k (T₁ − T₂) / L   [Heat Flux, W/m²]
Q = k A (T₁ − T₂) / L   [Heat Flow Rate, W]

🌡️ Temperature at a Point

For a uniform wall/rod with ends at T₁ (hot) and T₂ (cold), temperature at position x from the hot end:

T(x) = T₁ − [(T₁ − T₂) × x / L]

📋 Important Terms

  • k — Thermal conductivity (W/m·K): ability to conduct heat
  • q — Heat flux (W/m²): heat transferred per unit area
  • Q — Heat flow rate (W): total heat transferred per second
  • L — Thickness or length of the wall/rod (m)
  • A — Cross-sectional area (m²)
