Aim

Objective of the experiment

Experiment Objective

The aim of this virtual lab experiment is to study one dimensional heat conduction through a solid wall or rod and to calculate important parameters such as heat flux, rate of heat flow, thermal conductivity, and temperature at a particular point.

By completing this lab, students will be able to:

  • Understand the concept of one dimensional heat flow.
  • Apply Fourier's law of heat conduction.
  • Calculate heat flux and rate of heat transfer.
  • Determine thermal conductivity from known values.
  • Find temperature at any location in a wall under steady-state conditions.

Theory

Background concepts, formulas and video

One Dimensional Heat Conduction

When heat transfer takes place only in one direction, the process is called one dimensional heat conduction. It usually occurs through the thickness of a wall or along the length of a rod.

Fourier's Law

Fourier's law states that the rate of heat transfer is proportional to thermal conductivity, cross-sectional area, and temperature gradient.

Heat Flux: q = k (T₁ − T₂) / L

Rate of Heat Flow: Q = k A (T₁ − T₂) / L

Temperature Distribution

For a plane wall under steady-state conditions with constant thermal conductivity and no heat generation, temperature varies linearly with distance.

Temperature at a point x: T(x) = T₁ − [(T₁ − T₂) x / L]

Important Terms

  • Thermal conductivity (k): ability of a material to conduct heat.
  • Heat flux (q): heat transferred per unit area.
  • Heat flow rate (Q): total heat transferred per second.
  • Steady-state conduction: temperature at each point does not change with time.
